Academics

Your child looked at their real school results — not the version they may have shared with you. They looked honestly at which subjects are going well and which ones need more work.

They then wrote a SMART goal for this term — something Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant and Time-bound. A real target with a real deadline, not just "I will try harder."

For many of them, this was the first time they had been honest about a subject that is not going well.

Try this: Ask them to show you their SMART goal. Not to check it or judge it — just to know it. You can say: "I want to know what you are working toward."

Academic Literacy

READING YOUR
CHILD'S REPORT.

Your reference copy from today's session. Use it when the next report arrives.

Start with the small wins. Look first for a subject that improved, a mark at or above the class average, or a teacher comment that mentions effort. Start the conversation there — before the red lights.
CAPS Achievement Levels
Class average matters. 58% against a class average of 52% is above average. The same 58% against 72% is below. Always ask for context before reacting.

"Promoted" — requirements were met. "Progressed" — the system moved your child forward even though not all requirements were met. The gaps remain.

A student can only repeat a grade once in Grades 7–9. After that they are automatically progressed. This is mid-year. There is still time. A red light now is a warning, not a verdict.

The gateway to degree study at university. Required at Grade 12: 40% Home Language, 40% First Additional Language, 50% Mathematics (or 40% Maths Literacy), 50% in four other subjects.

Pure Maths keeps more doors open than Maths Literacy. The habits built in Grade 8 and 9 directly shape what is possible in Grade 12.

O
Outstanding — "Your teacher says your effort is outstanding. Tell me what that looks like."
G
Good — "Good effort — which subject do you work hardest in?"
S
Satisfactory — "You are meeting expectations. Where could you push a bit more?"
N
Needs improvement — "I want to understand what is getting in the way — not to judge you."
U
Unsatisfactory — "Something is wrong — not with you, with the situation. Let's figure it out together."

Not all schools use this system. Check the legend on the report — usually at the bottom or on the back.
